CloudShelf is a cloud storage product that caters to interior designers and architects. The product can view, upload, and edit your files real-time on the cloud.

View Desktop Prototype

The Challenge

Time, Money, and Preference

The cloud storage market is still young and there is room for a particular audience. CloudShelf wants to provide specific features and services, particularly for interior designers and architects. I was delighted to help with design and development. Many interior designers and architects would need to send out files through email every day and it takes lots of time to pack the files. This issue can be solved if there is a cloud for them to use. Interior designers and architects do lots of markup and redlines every day at work to communicate design development. The markup feature added to the cloud will help to solve that problem. Concept images and material references are all over the place online. It’s hard to collect everything and organize them well. This issue can be solved if they can simply copy the link and upload on the cloud to organize them.

The Solution

CloudShelf allows the users to share all the files through the link and provides the real-time markup service that solves the conflict on redlines. It allows users to collect and organize images, files, reference from other platforms.

Who are the users?

To gain a deeper understanding of my potential users, I interviewed them as they talked about their goals and frustrations. Not only did I confirm some of the survey results, but I also discovered that this pool of audience heavily values user-friendly designs — to the point where they determine whether to keep using an app or not within the first few minutes of the product experience.

Throughout the project, I continuously referred back to these personas to guide design decisions and priorities and to ensure that I was always thinking from their shoes, addressing their goals and frustrations.

The Strategy

Information Architecture, User Stories, User Flows

With the product concept and personas in mind, I began constructing the mobile sitemap, which laid the foundation and structure of CloudShelef. Followed by listing out all possible actions that make the MVP a truly user-friendly, time-saving, intuitive, and functional mobile app, I was able to prioritize and eliminate user stories while spotting issues in user flows and functionalities.
For me, this part of the process reinforced the product concept, scopes, and goals of the app.

Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

  • I want to upload a file(s) on the cloud platform
  • I want to save a file(s) from the URL link to the cloud platform
  • I want to share collaboration boards or documents
  • I want to real-time markup the file on the cloud platform
  • I want to edit or comment on collaborated files
  • I want to create the notes/presentation on the cloud platform

The Framework

Wireframe with Whimsical

Starting with wireframes, I was able to explore all of my ideas. I didn’t have to worry about anything else but the structure and how content was going to be laid out. Using Whimsical, I worked on refining the hand-drawn sketch, focused on hierarchy, design elements, as well as placements. I created multiple layouts for the same screen and asked for feedback. Although paper wireframes can communicate a whole lot by themselves, creating a mid-fidelity wireframe helped me gather more insightful feedback from potential users. I believe it is important never to ask my users to imagine things because it can sometimes go in a very different direction.


To do some testings before jumping into high-fidelity mockups, I decided to create an interactive prototype with Whimsical. I particularly tested the following flows:

  1. Sign up/ Sign in process
  2. Adding file(s)
  3. Sharing file(s)

Conclusion

Lessons Learned

If I had more time,

I would spend more time on research and ideation to explore different solutions. I want to investigate more on how designers organize their files differently based on storage product.
Real-time markup is a big thing in the architectural industry. I was careful not to eliminate the personal touch between interior designers, architects, and clients while I was creating this digital experience.

Appreciate and comprehend the work of others

There were times when I felt stuck working on the project. Doing the research and looking for inspirations havehelped me broaden my creative library and practice thinking critically about each solution designers came up with solving their challenge.
