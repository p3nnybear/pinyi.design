BusyBus is a transit app that provides the real-time information.

Frontend Development

The Challenge

Time, Accuracy, and Convenience

BusyBus is an application that allows commuters in a large city to search for the coming buses with real-time information. Riders are currently complaining the most about the bus stop at Washington and State, which has seven bus lines serving the stop.

The Solution

Simplified user interface for BusyBus to ensure the commuters know exactly when their Bus is arriving so that they can plan their commute.

The Discovery

Testing Waters

To know if there is a potential users for the transit app, I needed to gather insights about the user behavior and user experience.

Survey Analysis

People who did the survey are mostly 25-30 years old. There are designers, students, and tech professionals. I found that people would use the transit app because of several reasons: affordable, avoid traffic, a time convenient, avoid vehicle parking issues, etc. The benefit will be saving time and saving money. People who don’t like to take transportation because they think it’s dirty, too crowded and sometimes have bad schedules. People think less than 10 mins in advance they would arrive at the pick-up point. People who don’t use transportation because they drive and have their own vehicle.

Transportation Benefits

87% of the respondents who use the public transportation because they think it saves money and time

Expected Features

85% of the respondents want to see Real-time Information and estimated time of arrival with the transit app.

Usage

63% of the respondents think the public transportation is inconvenience.

Does BusyBus stand out?

To see where BusyBus would stand in the market, I researched 2 other transit application (Google Map and Moovit), registered an account for each, and analyzed how BusyBus would differentiate itself.
