A global lifestyle brand, NØVELTY was established in 2018. Renowned for its iconic fashion sense, NØVELTY has expanded to a full collection of ready-to-wear and accessories including shoes, handbags, small leather goods, scarves, and fine jewelry. In 2018, the founder decided to establish NØVELTY to share a unique style of an everyday look. Bringing finely curated international brands to you, we are proud to be your closet.​

View Mobile Prototype

The Challenge

Fashion, Trend, and User Friendly

I decided to found a luxury retail platform specifically catering to the Asian market. The reason is that there are luxury platforms selling designer brands in Europe and North America. However, no major platforms sell designer brands targeting Asian countries, with an experience tailored to the Asian consumer. I think there is the potential to start a similar platform in Asia.

The Solution

We discovered a vast untapped market in Asian countries like Taiwan, Japan, South Korea and China which means there is a niche and high potential sales. Mobile phones play an ever-increasing role in how we interact with our environment. Nowadays, most modern consumers are familiar with app shopping. Therefore, I sought to design a user-friendly app with a minimalistic aesthetic appealing to my target audience.

Who are the users?

To gain a deeper understanding of my potential users, I interviewed them as they talked about their goals and frustrations. Not only did I confirm some of the survey results, but I also discovered that this pool of audience heavily values user-friendly designs — to the point where they determine whether to keep using an app or not within the first few minutes of the product experience.

Throughout the project, I continuously referred back to these personas to guide design decisions and priorities and to ensure that I was always thinking from their shoes, addressing their goals and frustrations.

Does NØVELTY stand out?

To see where NØVELTY would stand in the market, I researched 3 other cloud service products (SSENSE, Farfetch, and Matchesfashion), registered an account for each, and analyzed how NØVELTY would differentiate itself. The key takeaway is that no such application exists in this market.

The Strategy

Information Architecture, User Stories, User Flows

With the product concept and personas in mind, I began constructing the mobile sitemap, which laid the foundation and structure of NØVELTY. Followed by listing out all possible actions that make the MVP a truly user-friendly, time-saving, intuitive, and functional mobile app, I was able to prioritize and eliminate user stories while spotting issues in user flows and functionalities. For me, this part of the process reinforced the product concept, scopes, and goals of the app.

The Framework

Wireframe with Whimsical

Starting with wireframes, I was able to explore all of my ideas. I didn’t have to worry about anything else but the structure and how content was going to be laid out. Using Whimsical, I worked on refining the hand-drawn sketch, focused on hierarchy, design elements, as well as placements. I created multiple layouts for the same screen and asked for feedback. Although paper wireframes can communicate a whole lot by themselves, creating a mid-fidelity wireframe helped me gather more insightful feedback from potential users. I believe it is important never to ask my users to imagine things because it can sometimes go in a very different direction.



To do some testings before jumping into high-fidelity mockups, I decided to create an interactive prototype with Whimsical. I particularly tested the following flows

  1. Sign up/ Sign in process
  2. Finding a specific item(s)
  3. Checkout process

The Experience

Identity, Logo, Visual Design

The NØVELTY logo is minimal symbol design with the novelty concept and the Ø combination. This powerful symbol represents the NØVELTY shopping experience.

Color Palette

NØVELTY uses minimal colors like white, black and shades of gray as the main color palette. It shows the luxury feeling for the users.

Typography

Montserrat is a geometric sans-serif typeface representative of the aesthetics, very simple and universal. It has an appearance of efficiency and forwardness in the design. It allows us to emphasize the voices on NØVELTY.

The Refinement

Testing & Iteration

Usability tests and metrics revealed that some of the process and design decisions I made were not as intuitive as I thought. Referring back to the personas, I knew that users heavily value a user-friendly product and would like to minimize the time spent on understanding it. As a result, I needed to iterate, test, and achieve measurable outcomes to overcome these product shortcomings.

Feedback ― The users expect to see how they get to the page.
